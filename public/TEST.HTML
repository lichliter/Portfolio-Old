Students vs. Lunch 1

Students vs. Lunch



pdf
College students almost always have a stressful relationship with lunch. Whether its the lack of time, the lack of options near class, or the lack of spaces for a nice social outing between classes. In this research, we attempted to find insights useful for companies attempting to appeal to college kids for lunch. We found delivery has room for improvement as well as there being a lack of food stops for those looking to relax between class.

  • Purpl.io 2

    Purpl.io



    website
    Purpl.io connects instructors with valuable feedback and insights from their students throughout the quarter as well as provides students with an engaging way to connect with one another outside the classroom.

    Students can ask, answer, and upvote questions relevant to classes. These features are available until the course instructor posts a midterm survey review, at which point they are prompted to submit feedback about the course to regain access to the forums. This feedback improves their experience going forward in that course for the rest of the quarter. Professors receive automatically generated reports about their students' opinions on their teaching, making the feedback loop beneficial to both sides.

  • Student dashboard at Purpl.io

    Empathic Systems Project 1

    Empathic Systems Project



    github
    Working with Professor Dinda and the rest of the Empathic Systems Project, I created an android application that showed an algorithm to accurately detect a shake versus other types of motion. The algorithm utilized a lowpass filter along with an fft to easily identify specific frequencies in motion of the phone that made sense for the shaking of a human.

    ESP is a project focused on creating a system cognizant of their user through the use of different types of sensors available to the device.
